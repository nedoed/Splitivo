# Design Philosophy: "Fractured Commerce"

## The Movement

"Fractured Commerce" is a visual philosophy that finds poetry in the moment of division. It honors the quiet ritual that happens at the end of every shared meal, every group trip, every split grocery run — the folding of a receipt, the reaching for phones, the quiet math of friendship. This philosophy treats that moment not as an awkward transaction, but as an act of trust rendered in thermal paper and coin.

## Form & Geometry

The work lives at the intersection of precision and imperfection. A clean vertical axis divides the composition with the authority of a surgeon's cut, yet the torn edge along that seam retains the organic irregularity of paper fibers — serrated, human, real. This duality is essential: the app's promise is mathematical fairness, but the context is always human warmth. Geometric coins — rendered as perfect circles with meticulous inner detail — float in calibrated orbits on either side of the tear, each one a small monument to different economies: Swiss francs with their cross, euros with their parallel bars, dollars with their crowned S. Every curve is the product of deep expertise, every alignment the work of a master craftsman who has refined the composition through countless iterations.

## Color & Material

The palette is restrained and considered: a warm off-white paper tone (never pure white — always with the faint warmth of thermal receipts that have lived briefly in a wallet), anchored by a deep confident indigo that serves as the brand's primary voice, accented with a single warm copper-gold that gives the coins their weight and metallic honesty. A whisper of muted sage or dusty rose may appear as tertiary accent, but only where the composition demands breath. No gradients that feel synthetic. No drop-shadows that shout. Every color has been painstakingly calibrated to feel tactile — as if the viewer could feel the fiber of paper and the cold smooth face of metal.

## Scale, Rhythm & Composition

The mark operates at two scales simultaneously: it must read with absolute clarity at 48 pixels on a home screen, and reveal its meticulous detail when rendered large. This requires surgical restraint — every ornament earns its place. The wordmark "Splitivo" sits with quiet confidence, ideally broken by the same vertical tear that cleaves the receipt, so the identity itself becomes an enactment of the product's function. The letterforms should feel slightly geometric, slightly friendly — never corporate, never childish. Rhythm comes from the repetition of circular coin forms balanced against the rectangular receipt body, a dialogue between hard edges and soft curves.

## Visual Hierarchy & Craftsmanship

Information is encoded entirely through form. No explanation required. The eye moves: from the silhouette of the receipt, to the shock of the division, to the symmetrical dispersal of coins, to the name itself — fractured and reunited. This is a mark that must look as though a master identity designer labored over every vertex, every tear-notch, every coin detail for weeks. It must have the inevitability of the best logos — that quality of feeling like it could not have been any other way. The final work should bear the fingerprints of painstaking attention: the kerning refined to the micrometer, the tear edge composed with the care of a botanical illustration, the coins carrying the quiet dignity of a numismatist's plate. Nothing cartoonish. Nothing loud. Everything resolved.

## The Silent Concept

Beneath the literal imagery lives the real subject: **trust between people**. The split is never an ending — it is the bookkeeping that allows friendship to continue unburdened. The two halves of the receipt are not fighting each other; they are partners. The coins on each side are equal in number and weight. The typography that breaks and rejoins across the seam is a visual promise: things divided do not lose their wholeness.
